Lesson 1: The <p> tag

The <P> Tag Is One Of the greatest in the website designers toolbox, infact you are reading text created by it right now!
If You havn't Guessed Already the <p> Tag Makes A Paragraph On The Webpage, The "<" At the Begging And end of it signal
The Computer That it is a tag, if you dont use them then your code will not work.
Lets Add One To Our Code Right Now, Put A <p> Tag Right Below Your <Body> Tag:


<!DOCTYPE html>
<html>
<head>
<meta charset="utf-8">
<title>Example</title>
</head>
<body>
<p>
</body>
</html>


Now Put Any Text After It.
Hopefully It Worked But Now Another Issue Arises "How Do I Create Line Breaks?" You can't Just Press Enter Because The Computer
Dosnt Recongnize that. In This Case We Need:

Lesson 2: The <br> tag

If You Want To Create A Line Break In Your Text You Could Either:

  • Use A </p> to End The Paragraph (more on that later)
  • Use The <br> Tag to Create A Linebreak
  • Give Up and Face defeat

In My Personal Opinion I Would Use The <br> Tag But Ending The Paragraph and starting a new one also works.
Here Is An Example Of How To Use A Line Break:

<!DOCTYPE html>
<html>
<head>
<meta charset="utf-8">
<title>Example</title>
</head>
<body>
<p>Jhon slamed The Door Shut <br> He Was So Mad That He Was Beaten By A 3rd Grader
</body>
</html>

Here's What That Would Look Like:

Jhon slamed The Door Shut
He Was So Mad That He Was Beaten By A 3rd Grader

Lesson 3: Ending tags

Remember How Earlier I was talking About ending the paragraph and starting a new one instead of using a line break?
Adding a "/" before the letters in a tag creates an ending tag, Most tags need these to function proporly
One Of the only tags that doesnt is the <br> Tag. This is Usefull For things such as adding headers and many other HTML
Tags Afterward. Having A Ending Tag can also Make A New Paragraph Just like the <br> Tag although it is much easier to use the
<br> Tag.
Here Is an example of when you could use an ending tag instead of a <br> tag:

<!DOCTYPE html>
<html>
<head>
<meta charset="utf-8">
<title>Example</title>
</head>
<body>
<p>Jhon slamed The Door Shut <br> He Was So Mad That He Was Beaten By A 3rd Grader</p>
<p>"Are you okay up There?" His Mom Called</p>
<p>"Doing Great" Jhon said Sarcasticly</p>
</body>
</html>

Here's What it would look like:

Jhon slamed The Door Shut
He Was So Mad That He Was Beaten By A 3rd Grader

"Are you okay up There?" His Mom Called

"Doing Great" Jhon said Sarcasticly

Lesson 4: Headers

So Right Now Our text is kinda Small, And Say i want to make a book. I need A Title and A Chapter Title.
Lets Add A Header To Our Webpage. You Can Use The <h1> Tag for that.
The 1 In <h1> Is THe Size, 1 is the biggest 6 is the smallest. Im Going to add a title And A Chapter To My Code:
